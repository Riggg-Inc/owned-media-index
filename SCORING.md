# Scoring

The Owned Media Index scores patterns, tools, benchmarks, and standards from 1 to 5.

Scores are not popularity votes. They are editorial judgments based on usefulness, evidence, repeatability, and fit for real production.

## Score Definitions

### 5 - Recommended Default

Use this as the default for serious owned media programs.

Criteria:

- Strong fit for common use cases.
- Repeatable across multiple programs.
- Clear quality bar.
- Strong evidence or internal production validation.
- Tradeoffs are known and manageable.

### 4 - Strong Fit

Use regularly, with some context-specific judgment.

Criteria:

- Works well in common scenarios.
- Strong practical value.
- Some tradeoffs or narrower fit than a 5.
- Evidence is good but may not be benchmark-backed yet.

### 3 - Viable

Works, but only with clear tradeoffs.

Criteria:

- Useful in some cases.
- Requires operator judgment.
- May depend on team skill, budget, format, or channel.
- Evidence may be practitioner observation rather than benchmark data.

### 2 - Niche

Good for a narrow situation.

Criteria:

- Specific fit only.
- Not a default recommendation.
- Tradeoffs are significant.
- Useful enough to document, but not broadly recommended.

### 1 - Not Recommended

Included for context, comparison, or warning.

Criteria:

- Poor default choice.
- Outdated, risky, unreliable, or low-value.
- May still be useful for historical context or edge cases.

## Evidence Levels

Every scored entry should identify its evidence level:

- **Hypothesis:** plausible but not yet tested.
- **Practitioner observation:** based on production experience or expert judgment.
- **Internal data:** supported by Riggg or customer production data.
- **External research:** supported by credible public research or platform documentation.
- **Benchmark-backed:** supported by repeatable performance benchmarks.

## Updating Scores

Scores should change when:

- Platform behavior changes.
- Better patterns emerge.
- Tool pricing or reliability changes.
- New benchmark data contradicts prior assumptions.
- Customer production data shows a stronger or weaker fit.
