# Descriptions

**Stage:** Package

Descriptions are the second packaging decision after titles. They appear in RSS feeds, podcast apps, YouTube, social posts, and website show notes. A strong description helps the audience decide whether to invest their time.

## Description Styles

| Style | Best For | Score |
|---|---|---|
| [Problem-Credibility-Takeaway](problem-credibility-takeaway.md) | Expert interviews with clear outcomes | 5 |
| [Big Question Summary](big-question-summary.md) | Thought leadership, exploratory episodes | 4 |
| [Audience Pain Breakdown](audience-pain-breakdown.md) | Tactical, solution-oriented episodes | 4 |
| [Guest Story Arc](guest-story-arc.md) | Narrative-driven interviews | 4 |
| [Thesis-Takeaways-CTA](thesis-takeaways-cta.md) | Concise, high-signal episodes | 4 |

## Quality Bar

A strong description should:

- Open with a sentence that earns the second sentence
- Establish guest credibility or topic relevance quickly
- Tell the listener what they will learn — specifically
- Include key takeaways, timestamps, or chapter markers where appropriate
- End with a clear CTA (subscribe, visit, follow)
- Work within platform truncation limits (first 120 chars matter most)
