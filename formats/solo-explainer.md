# Solo Explainer

Host teaches one concept end to end.

## What This Is For

Solo explainers are for point-of-view content, frameworks, tactical lessons, and direct teaching.

## When To Use It

Use this format when:

- The host has a clear thesis.
- Production needs to be lightweight.
- The audience needs a framework or explanation.
- The episode can stand without a guest.

## What Good Looks Like

Strong solo explainers include:

- Concept outline.
- Clear single thesis.
- Visual aid checklist where relevant.
- Recap CTA.
- 15 to 30 minute optimal length.
