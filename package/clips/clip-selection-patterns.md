# Clip Selection Patterns

## What It Is

A set of repeatable patterns for choosing moments from long-form owned media that can stand alone as short-form clips.

## Best For

- Podcast video clips.
- Webinar highlights.
- Virtual event takeaways.
- LinkedIn posts.
- YouTube Shorts, Reels, and TikTok.

## Why It Works

Good clips are not just short excerpts. They are complete enough to create value without the full episode, while still pointing back to the larger asset.

## Example Patterns

1. **The Sharp Take:** a clear opinion or thesis.
2. **The Useful Explanation:** a concept explained simply.
3. **The Mistake:** a common error and how to avoid it.
4. **The Before/After:** transformation from one state to another.
5. **The Story Moment:** a concise anecdote with a lesson.
6. **The Data Point:** a surprising number or benchmark.
7. **The Framework:** a named model or process.
8. **The Objection:** a common objection answered directly.
9. **The Warning:** a risk the audience should avoid.
10. **The Invitation:** a moment that naturally leads to the full episode.

## Quality Bar

A clip should have a beginning, middle, and end. It should not feel like a random excerpt.

## When Not To Use

Avoid clipping moments that depend too heavily on missing context, private details, or visual references the viewer cannot understand.

## Riggg Score

5

## Evidence

Evidence level: practitioner observation.

Needs benchmark data from retention, completion rate, clicks, and comments.
