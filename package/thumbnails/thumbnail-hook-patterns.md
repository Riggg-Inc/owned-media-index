# Thumbnail Hook Patterns

## What It Is

Reusable patterns for thumbnail concepts that help an owned media asset earn attention without misleading the audience.

## Best For

- YouTube episodes.
- Webinar replays.
- Virtual event sessions.
- Website episode pages.

## Why It Works

Thumbnails are visual positioning. They should help the viewer understand the topic, tone, and stakes before reading the title.

## Example Patterns

1. **The Tension:** visualize the conflict or problem.
2. **The Expert:** clear guest/host face with topic cue.
3. **The Outcome:** show the promised result.
4. **The Mistake:** highlight what to avoid.
5. **The Comparison:** before/after or old/new.
6. **The Framework:** show the model or named idea.
7. **The Question:** pair a strong question with a relevant visual.
8. **The Moment:** use an expressive still from the conversation.
9. **The Object:** anchor the concept in a recognizable tool, chart, or artifact.
10. **The Event:** signal live/session context for webinars or virtual events.

## Quality Bar

The thumbnail should communicate the asset's point at small size.

## When Not To Use

Avoid overly busy thumbnails, misleading emotion, tiny text, and generic stock imagery.
