# OBS Real-Time Production Standard

## What It Is

A standard for using OBS or equivalent live production software to create show-style rough edits during or immediately around the recording process.

## Best For

- Podcasts with video.
- Live shows.
- Webinars.
- Virtual event sessions.
- Programs that need fast turnaround.

## Why It Works

Real-time production moves production decisions upstream. Lower thirds, titles, overlays, transitions, intro/outro moments, and layout choices happen before the asset reaches final mastering.

This reduces post-production overhead and raises the quality floor.

## Required Elements

- Scene plan.
- Branded overlays.
- Lower thirds.
- Intro/outro sequence.
- Horizontal layout.
- Vertical layout where needed.
- Audio monitoring.
- Backup recording.
- Producer checklist.

## Quality Bar

The rough edit should already feel like a produced show before final mastering begins.

## When Not To Use

Avoid heavy real-time production when:

- The session is exploratory and structure would get in the way.
- The customer needs only raw archival capture.
- The production team cannot monitor quality live.
