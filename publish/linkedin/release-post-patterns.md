# LinkedIn Release Post Patterns

## What It Is

Reusable patterns for publishing new owned media assets on LinkedIn.

## Best For

- B2B podcasts.
- Webinar recaps.
- Founder-led media.
- Expert interviews.
- Virtual event highlights.

## Why It Works

LinkedIn posts need to translate the media asset into a feed-native reason to care. The post should not simply announce that an episode exists.

## Example Patterns

1. **The Lesson:** lead with one useful insight from the episode.
2. **The Tension:** name the problem the conversation resolves.
3. **The Guest POV:** highlight a sharp guest perspective.
4. **The Clip Companion:** introduce the idea shown in the attached clip.
5. **The Operator Takeaway:** turn the conversation into an actionable lesson.
6. **The Contrarian Hook:** challenge a common assumption.
7. **The Event Recap:** turn a session into a concise recap with link.
8. **The Question:** ask the central question the episode answers.
9. **The Quote Anchor:** build the post around one strong quote.
10. **The Resource Drop:** frame the episode as a practical resource.

## Quality Bar

The post should be valuable even before someone clicks.

## When Not To Use

Avoid posts that only say "new episode is live" unless the audience already has strong demand.
